On the half-life of conviction

Most strong opinions have a shorter shelf life than we admit. The interesting practice is not holding convictions, but noticing the moment they start to decay — and deciding whether to reinforce or release them.

The founders who navigate this well tend to update fast on evidence but slow on identity. They change what they think without changing who they are.

2026.02.10

Density as a product

The most valuable thing a community can produce is not content or connections but density — the compression of relevant people and ideas into a small enough space that collisions become inevitable. Everything else is a side effect of getting the density right.

2026.02.06

Selection vs. curation

Selection is about filtering. Curation is about sequencing. A good dinner party isn't just the right people — it's the right people in the right order of introduction. Most community builders optimize for the guest list when they should optimize for the choreography.
